There is a reason NauticPlayer exists: macOS needed a lightweight, distraction-free player for local music that doesn't hide the music's structure. Built for producers, DJs, and audiophiles, NauticPlayer lets you see exactly where the drop hits, analyze the break, and navigate your tracks instantly—without the bloat of a full DJ software.

### ✨ Main Features

* 🌊 **Interactive Waveform Display:** Instantly visualize track structure. Spot intros, breaks, and drops at a glance with a pristine, high-resolution waveform.
* 🪟 **Status Bar Integration:** True minimalism. Control your music seamlessly directly from the macOS top menu bar without switching windows or interrupting your workflow.
* 🎛️ **Dual Audio Engine:** Fluid mixes and automatic crossfade for a seamless listening experience.
* 🎹 **DAW Integration:** Drag files directly from the player's waveform straight into Logic Pro, Ableton, or FL Studio.

> [!WARNING]
> **macOS Gatekeeper First-Launch Guide**
> 
> Because NauticPlayer is an independent software, macOS will show an "Unidentified Developer" prompt on the first launch. Simply follow these steps to bypass it:
> 
> 1. Drag the app to your **Applications** folder.
> 2. **DO NOT double-click**. Instead, <kbd>Right-Click</kbd> (or <kbd>Control</kbd> + Click) on the NauticPlayer icon.
> 3. Select **"Open"** from the drop-down menu.
> 4. A confirmation window will appear. Click the **"Open"** button.
> 5. *(If the option doesn't appear)* Go to **System Settings > Privacy & Security** and click **"Open Anyway"**.
